How the coronavirus pandemic is affecting the mental health of people across the Scotland

It is an undeniable fact that the coronavirus epidemic has caused serious changes in every individual's life. Being a social being, the obligation to stay at home directly affects mental health by causing negative social and economic changes in their lives. This situation affects people from different segments of society at different levels of severity. Some have serious health problems, some are unemployed, some cannot go to school, some just feel lonely. As a result, even if everyone's method of dealing with problems in life is different, everyone's mental health suffered to a certain extent during this period. Predictably,differences in people's responses to the pandemic are not randomly distributed in the UK - they are due to people's social and economic position in society. Groups affected by socio-economic inequalities are more likely to report experiencing anxiety, panic, despair, loneliness, and being unable to cope well with the stress of the pandemic. The differentiation in the mental health effects of COVID-19 is of serious concern. Every individual in need should not hesitate to get help and the state should provide all kinds of support.
